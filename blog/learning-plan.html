What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

Ideally I would like to be able to ‘have choices’, as in having the ability to be location flexible and able to work fully remotely. I view the tech industry as a fantastic enabler for this lifestyle, as well as being an industry that is constantly evolving and changing.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

A key strength of mine is that I have always enjoyed the process of learning and exploring new concepts and Ideas. Once I find a topic interesting, I really want to explore it and find out more. This has benefits and weaknesses though, and sometimes I have to remind myself to stay organised and focused on a task instead of skimming the surface on a bunch of areas. Something else I am working on is self-comparison to others. I have to remind myself that it's okay when I see someone who is more knowledgeable or further ahead on a learning journey, and accept myself as I currently am.

What skills (non technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to learn to be more accepting and open with myself, and in turn, become a better communicator with others. Also, practising resilience and discipline when the course gets difficult/and or stressful.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles

I think having some prior life experience is beneficial and an asset, both in terms of personal development, as well as keeping perspective during the course. Over the years I have had a chance to get to know my own style of learning, workflow and identify strategies that work for me.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

If I get stuck on a problem and I cannot manage to find something to ‘unblock myself’ via google (like on Stack Overflow), I will try and communicate with others for help/to see if I am approaching the issue in the right way. Other strategies include taking a short break and coming back to look at the problem with ‘fresh eyes’ to hopefully process and rephrase the issue.

Description of what you expect from the facilitation team

Aside from general support, checking in from time to time to make sure everything is okay/voice any issues or concerns.

Any Scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

I am pretty loose on having ‘a strict schedule’, but I generally try and make sure I get a couple of hours of down-time in the evening before bed, as this is important for recharging myself and avoiding burnout.
