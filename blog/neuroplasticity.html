Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity, initially theorized from the late 1800s, relates to the malleability of the brain and its ability to constantly change and adapt from day to day experience. By actively engaging the brain in undertaking new learning, thought and information we can actively form new neural pathways and build connections.

Traditionally, this plasticity was thought to occur only during childhood, but in more recent years this school of thought has trained significantly, and research has shown that many aspects of the brain are able to be altered throughout adulthood.

This can have benefits in treatment and recovery programs for people who have suffered from brain injuries or strokes, as well as forming functional and behavioral tools and strategies to adapt for those who experience conditions such as ADD, Autism or Dyslexia.

Fixed vs. Growth

Conventional school of thought was much along the line that Intelligence was ‘set’ and that there was little that could be done to alter or improve functioning mental capacity. The great news is modern research shows this is far from the truth!

Internalised beliefs and attitudes have a big impact on how we perceive ourselves and interact with the world around us.

Fixed Mindset

A fixed mindset is one where you believe that qualities like intelligence, skill and ability to improve are relatively set and that is a limited ability to improve. Thoughts such as “Oh I’m not a numbers person, I can’t do that” may be encountered when encountering a problem that requires some mathematical input. The person may believe it is very hard or futile to attempt to improve at that skill, as they are just ‘not good at it’.

Fixed Mindset:

"Oh, I'm just not good at Math."

Growth Mindset

A growth mindset welcomes change and embraces challenges. It recognises that we have the ability to adapt, learn and improve and that if we are encountered with a problem that doesn’t feel familiar or comfortable, it is an opportunity to work and improve at that skill. This also swings the other way. A growth mindset realises that skills that are already familiar can still be improved upon and improved to be even better.

Growth Mindset

"Math is challenging for me now, but I can work and improve at it."

Outro

There are many benefits to cultivating a growth mindset in day to day tasks. How we perceive ourselves on both a conscious and subconscious level has a real impact in our approach, effort and persistence in the face of adversity. If we embrace this challenge as a chance to improve and better ourselves, we open ourselves to a whole new realm of opportunity.
