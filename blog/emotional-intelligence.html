Emotional Intelligence

Intelligence is a complex and wonderful thing that encompasses many different behaviors with how one processes and manages information. Abstraction, logic, learning, reasoning, creativity, problem solving and critical thinking are all expressions of Intelligence in different formats.


There is ‘no one size fits all’, and it is difficult to pin down one concrete definition of what makes up intelligence. In this blog, we will explore Emotional Intelligence (EQ) and why it is important to cultivate.

A look at the differences between EQ and IQ

Quite often, these terms are grouped together or even mistaken for one another. While they do share a lot of similarities, generally IQ is a more narrow-focused view of one’s own ability to take input of information and develop ration, logic, problem solving and analysis to form an output of knowledge. This is observed throughout the animal kingdom and even plants, as we adapt to strategies to better survive and thrive in our environment.


Emotional intelligence takes this concept and relates it to our ability to form self-awareness, understanding and collaboration with others. Humans are social animals, and our ability to form effective social connections and bond with others is a key essence of our development as a species.


Research shows that many of the strongest factors for success come from emotional intelligence, not intelligence quotient.


These higher concepts of ‘success-factors’ such as leadership, self-confidence, initiative and influence are built upon core fundamental skills of Emotional Intelligence. Being able to develop trust, to listen effectively, to both appreciate and be liked by others are at the core of this, and are crucial to bond with others and develop a healthy mindset of happiness and fulfillment.

Techniques to work at improving EQ

Attentive listening, mindfulness and Journal are some techniques to develop and improve emotional intelligence. Mindfulness has been shown to both improve attention and focus, as well as relieve anxiety. Forming a calm and stable sense of one’s self gives a great foundation to be trusting and open with others.


There are many benefits to improving emotional intelligence, and in today’s working environment being able to work, collaborate and communicate with others is a crucial skill to develop. Hopefully by working at this, we can all improve and just become a little bit better versions of ourselves.
